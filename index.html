Driven by the Pursuit of Discovery

Sage Advice

"The more you learn, the more you realize there is so much more to know. Never stop learning. Never stop growing". ... guiding wisdom from my father.

Here I am

Hello. My name is bW and I am embarking on a new professional endeavor - software develpment, specifically related to analytics and interactive data visualization for financial, engineering and scientific organizations. Yeh, I know it's a really specific objective, but I'm leveraging a former professional life in quantitative and financial analysis.

Kansas

At my core, I am a country boy from Kansas - a proud son of Osawatomie (a town of about 2,500 and maybe 5 stop lights). It was here that I was molded with Midwestern values and developed an 'uninformed' interest in finance and banking simply because the father of my best friend was president of the local bank.

Chicago

I suppose I was destined for big things because my parents decided to relocate to Chicago, IL during my first year of high school. Can you say "culture shock"! Well, I grew to love the "Windy City", the "City of Big Shoulders", Chi-town". I even became a die-hard Cubs fan.

New York City and Beyond

Having made the adjustment to Chicago, I decided that there was more of the world to see. So after graduating from college I thought I would go big again and relocated to New York City to take a job on Wall Street. Beyond my grunt job as a newbie 'sweat-dog' financial analyst, it was a period of fun and exploration that took me to California, back to Chicago twice and travels around the world. Most importantly, my work in finance was a key factor in my abiding interest in technology which had become integral to my work. In fact, the CEO of my company would often state that JPMorgan Chase was a technology company masquerading as a financial services organization.

Time for a Change, in Pursuit of a Passion

Eventually the big lights and fast life of the big city began to wear on me, and I yearned for the simpler existance of my Midwestern roots. I was ready for a change that would allow me to itch the technology bug while leveraging my quantitative DNA and skillset. A series of mostly fortunate events took me to Chicago (again) and then to St. Louis. I landed in a role as the business manager of an engineering firm, which primarilly entails developing a finance function and data analytics capability. (Engineering firms generate lots and lots of data in many disparate forms). This is my current challenge, requiring that I up my software development game rather than rely on 'off-the-shelf' solutions. Be careful what you ask for...

There is Balance

While I am intensely focused on building a foundation my budding software development career, I do have other interests that provide balance. As a native country boy, I am an adventurous outdoorsman. I very much enjoy and engage in any activity related to water and mountains. Kayaking is a favorite, and I spend many days each year in the Smoky or Rocky mountain regions. I am also an avid golfer, but I golf moreso for the serenitiy that comes with walking a beautifully designed and manicured course that seamlessly integrates the natural surroundings of the course.
